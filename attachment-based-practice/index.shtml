Next course date: Attachment-Based Practice with Adults

24-26 April 2018 at Novotel, Broad Street, Birmingham B1 2HT, UK

A course for professionals from a range of disciplines who work with parents, carers or other adults whose behaviour may place children or other adults at risk of significant harm. All delegates will receive a copy of Attachment-based Practice with Adults, by Clark Baim and Tony Morrison, published 2011 by Pavilion (retail price £95).

Fee: £450 (inclusive of VAT & lunches)

  What the course offers:

  • Understanding the key elements of contemporary attachment theory
  • Identifying how attachment strategies are expressed in speech and behaviour
  • Developing interview skills to assess the nature of the attachment strategy
  • Learning and practising a five-stage protocol to improve psychological coherence
  • Applying theory in practice through transcript analysis of clinical interviews
  • A comprehensive guide on attachment based interviewing, with detailed case studies

The course is a mixture of presentation, audio and video clips, and small group tasks.


  Who is this course for?

This course is for professionals with some experience of undertaking assessments or therapeutic work with parents or other adults who pose a risk to themselves or others. This includes those working in child welfare / child protection settings, adult mental health or counselling services, criminal justice and multi-disciplinary contexts that cross adult and children's services. The course is also relevant for professionals who supervise staff in the above areas.

  What the course offers:

The course is a mixture of presentation and small group tasks.


  Who is this course for?

The pre-requisite to attend this course is attendance at a Part One course in attachment-based practice with adults. This course is for professionals who work with parents or other adults who pose a risk to themselves or others. This includes those working in child welfare / child protection settings, safeguarding, adult mental health, social care or counselling services, criminal justice and multi-disciplinary contexts that cross adult and children's services. This course is also relevant for professionals who supervise staff in the above areas.
